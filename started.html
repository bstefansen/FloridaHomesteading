Getting Started

When starting out, it is important to do your research. When doing your research, you will soon learn that asking 30 beekeepers will give you 30 different answers. Regardless, most beekeepers will agree on the basics. For beginners, the best time to adopt a hive is in spring when most plants are blooming.

Equipment

When starting out, you really only need five basic items: a smoker, a hive, a bee colony, a suit, and a scraper. There are multiple suppliers and retailers throughout Florida, so it is only a matter of preference.

Smoker
A smoker is a metal container that generates smoke, which masks the alarm pheromones released by honeybees. This calms the bees down, and makes working with them a more pleasant experience.
Hive
The most popular form of hive is the langstroth hive, which consists of a brood box (where the queen lays eggs) and a honey super (where the bees store excess honey). Starting out, you will need one of each.
Others
You will also need the bees themselves! These can purchased through various sellers across Florida. You will also need a suit (so you don't get stung), and a scraper to pry open the hive during inspections.

Education

This website has only scratched the surface of all there is to know. The best beekeepers are those who have been around the most bees, and by continually learning, you become a better beekeeper. Once you feel confident in taking that first step, you will soon learn what a wonderful addition beekeeping is to your homestead.
