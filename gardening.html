What is Gardening?

Gardening is the act of growing and cultivating plants. Although this may seem trivial, it requires extensive knowledge and practice to be done properly on a Florida homestead

Why Garden?

Due to Florida's unique climate, it is possible to grow vegetables year round if planting dates are properly managed. By gardening, you are able to enjoy the fresh air, provide fresh and healthy food for you and your family, and save money on grocery bills.

Getting Started

You will want to begin your garden by picking an ideal location. This location should be close to your house for convenience, have well-draining soil, and offer plenty of sunshine throughout the day. You will now need to prepare your soil. While using the soil you have is fine, it is worthwhile to add compost and/or organic matter for additional nutrients. To learn more about composting, click here. Once your soil is ready, you can now choose the plants you wish to grow. Take note of the growing zones chart provided by the USDA on this page. A great resource for planting dates in Florida can be found here. Follow the watering intervals provided, and you will soon enjoy the fruits of your labor!
