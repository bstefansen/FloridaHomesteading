What is Rainwater Harvesting?

RWH is the practice of harvesting, collecting, storing, and distributing rainwater runoff. Not only will you save money on your water bill, but you will aid in the fight for water conservation.

Why Harvest Rainwater?

By harvesting rainwater, we are reducing our strain on municipality systems and reducing operation costs for our property and the city. Despite the initial expenses for setting up your system, these techniques provide a clean and free source of water for a variety of homestead applications. If you are using it for drinking, make sure to use some form of external filter to prevent waterborne diseases. Otherwise, feel free to hook it up to your home's water system or hose for irrigating your property.

Getting Started

The first thing you will need is some form of a container that is both waterproof and made of food grade material. Now, attach some form of a valve, spicket, or opening near the bottom of the container. You will then need to find a way to get rainwater to your container. The most popular method involves installing a downspout on your roof gutters and placing the container underneath. There are various methods to choose from, all of which depend on your budget and location.

Maintenance

Maintaining a rainwater harvesting system is important for upholding water quality on your homestead. Metal grates and meshes should be installed and cleaned in your downspout to prevent large debris and leaves from entering your container. If the water is used for drinking, make sure to use a filter that screens pathogens and heavy metals. Your container should also have a mosquito net on the opening to prevent breeding, especially in Florida. Follow these guidelines and you will be ready to harness rain on your homestead.
