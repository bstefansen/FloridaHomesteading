What is Beekeeping?

Despite being practiced for thousands of years, the art of beekeeping continues to be the cornerstone of a thriving and self-sufficient homestead in Florida. Not only will these insects benefit your garden, but you will be rewarded with honey for your efforts.

Why Beekeeping?

By cultivating honeybees on your homestead, you are contributing to the conservation of an important agricultural pollinator. In addition to this, honeybees provide both pollen transport and delicious honey for your homestead. When you finally introduce these insects to your property, they will contribute to a superior harvest and a healthier ecosystem.

History

Honeybees are actually not native to America, but were introduced through European colonists in the 17th century. Unfortunately, no one knows when the first honeybee arrived to Florida. During the late 19th century when citrus production in Florida began, reports of large honey yields began to spread. Due to the lack of infrastructure and roadways within Florida at the time, beekeeping had a slow beginning from what it is today. At the turn of the century, beekeeping and the agricultural industry began to develop, leading Florida to continually be in the top 5 honey-producing states. Today, there are nearly 5,000 registered beekeepers in the state of Florida.
